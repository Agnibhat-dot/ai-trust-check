The Problem

AI models like ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini are powerful, but they make mistakes:

  • ⚠️ Hallucinations - Making up facts that sound plausible
  • ⚠️ Outdated Info - Training data cutoffs mean wrong recent facts
  • ⚠️ Unsupported Claims - Statements without evidence or sources
  • ⚠️ Overconfidence - Presenting uncertain info as definite
  • ⚠️ Bias - Skewed perspectives based on training data

Our Solution

Frequently Asked Questions

What AI models can I verify?

You can verify responses from any AI model: ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Copilot, Llama, and others. Our verifier is model-agnostic and analyzes the response content itself.

Why should I trust this verification?

Our verification uses multiple techniques to identify claims and red flags. However, it's a first-pass analysis tool. Always use critical thinking and verify important claims independently.

Is my text stored or logged?

No. Your input is processed instantly and not stored. We respect your privacy completely. No data is logged or shared.

How accurate is the trust score?

The trust score is indicative and based on pattern analysis. Use it as a guide, not absolute truth. Critical thinking is essential.

Can I verify code or technical content?

Yes, the verifier works on any text content including code, technical documentation, research summaries, and more.

What's considered a hallucination?

Hallucinations are unsupported statements presented as fact. They're claims that lack evidence, contradictory information, or plausible-sounding but false details.
